## EN — Hard requirements (stable + [verify])

### Profile and language
- **Languages:** German primarily; English and French also accepted. Other
  languages — `[verify]`.
- Publisher: **De Gruyter** (Berlin). Peer-reviewed.
- Field: Oriental studies — Ancient Near East, Egyptology, Islamic studies,
  Indology, East Asian and Inner Asian studies; Semitic and classical
  philology of the East.

### Genre — distinctive feature
- OLZ is primarily a **review journal**. Three main genres:
  1. **Book reviews (Rezensionen)** — the bulk of the journal; typically
     1–6 printed pages.
  2. **Review articles (Sammelrezensionen / Forschungsberichte)** — extended
     overviews of a topic or set of works.
  3. **Articles (Aufsätze)** — less frequent than reviews; full research articles.
- The genre dictates structure, length and citation density — `[verify]` for
  each.

### Length
- No single hard cap — varies by genre. `[verify]` per Instructions for
  Authors.

### Book-review heading
- A review begins with a **bibliographic header** of the work reviewed:
  Author. *Title*. (Series, if any.) Place: Press, Year. Pp. xx + xxx + N
  illustrations. ISBN. Price.
  Exact punctuation / order — `[verify]` against De Gruyter examples.

### Transliteration
- Standard scholarly Latinisation per language: Indology — IAST or ISO 15919
  (`[verify]` preferred); Akkadian / Sumerian — standard Assyriological
  systems; Arabic / Persian — DMG (Deutsche Morgenländische Gesellschaft)
  conventions are common in German-language oriental studies and likely the
  default — `[verify]`. Preserve diacritics exactly.

### Citations
- **Footnote-based**, per common De Gruyter / German philological practice —
  `[verify]` exact form (short footnote on second mention; first mention
  full or per a separate bibliography).

### Bibliography / References
- For Aufsätze: a list at the end — `[verify]` whether expected as separate
  bibliography or via complete first-cite footnotes.
- For reviews: usually no separate bibliography (citations in footnotes only).
- Non-Latin titles: transliteration + bracketed translation + language tag.

### Author metadata
- Author name, affiliation, e-mail — `[verify]` whether ORCID is required.

### Review model
- Peer-reviewed; specific model (single- or double-blind) — `[verify]`.

### Language register
- Cautious scholarly German register; for review submissions, evaluate the
  work substantively (strengths, weaknesses, contribution) without
  ad hominem or sensationalist tone.

## RU — Краткое резюме

OLZ — старейший европейский востоковедческий журнал (с 1898 г.), издаваемый
**De Gruyter** (Берлин). **Преимущественно рецензионный**: большинство
публикаций — книжные рецензии 1–6 стр., плюс обзорные и исследовательские

для OLZ.
