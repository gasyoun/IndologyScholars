## EN — Hard requirements (stable + [verify])

### Profile and language
- **Language:** English (historically also German and French — `[verify]`
  current policy).
- Publisher: **American Oriental Society**; published quarterly. Peer-reviewed.
- Field: Oriental studies broadly — Near East, Islamic world, South Asia, East
  Asia, ancient and modern; philological, literary, historical, religious.

### Length
- No public hard cap by characters — `[verify]`. Articles, communications, and
  book reviews have different scope; consult the style sheet.

### Abstract
- English abstract (`[verify]` length, typically short ~150–200 words for AOS
  journals).

### Transliteration — *the* distinctive JAOS feature
- JAOS maintains a **style sheet with dedicated transliteration tables for each
  language**: Sanskrit, Pali, Prakrits (IAST), Avestan, Old Persian, Akkadian,
  Sumerian, Arabic, Persian, Ottoman, Hebrew, Syriac, Ethiopic, Tibetan,
  Mongolian, Chinese, Japanese, Korean, and others. Do **not** assume a single
  romanisation system; use the JAOS table for the language at hand — `[verify]`
  the current edition of the style sheet.
- Preserve diacritics exactly; consistency within an article is mandatory.

### Citations
- **Footnote-based** citation system (not in-text author-date), per common AOS
  practice — `[verify]` exact form. Typical short form in footnotes is
  `Author Year, page` (or author + short title + page) with a full bibliography
  at the end of the article.
- Discursive notes also go in footnotes.

### Bibliography / References
- A **single References list** at the end (no separate Latin duplicate is
  needed; the apparatus is already in Latin script).
- Format follows the JAOS style sheet — `[verify]`. For non-Latin titles
  (e.g. Russian, Arabic), give transliteration + bracketed English translation
  + language tag `(in Russian)` etc.

### Author metadata
- Author name and affiliation; e-mail and ORCID — `[verify]` requirement.

### Illustrations
- Separate files at publication-quality resolution — `[verify]` specifics
  (typical: 300 dpi minimum, TIFF/EPS, with captions and permission notes).

### Review
- Peer-reviewed; review model (single- or double-blind) — `[verify]`.

## RU — Краткое резюме

JAOS — старейший западный востоковедческий журнал (с 1843 г.), издаваемый
Американским восточным обществом. Уникальная черта — **детальный style sheet
с транслитерационными таблицами по каждому языку**; единой системы нет.
Цитирование — **через постраничные сноски** (а не author-date в скобках),
библиография в конце статьи. Для нелатинских источников — транслитерация +
перевод в квадратных скобках + пометка языка. Точные правила длины,
аннотации, форматирования сносок и иллюстраций — строго **`[verify]`** по
актуальному JAOS Style Sheet.

> Differs from IIJ (author-date) in using **footnotes** for citations; differs
> from ППВ in being English-language with no Cyrillic-script Литература block.
