# Guide to Verifying RINC/eLIBRARY Profiles (SciGuide Phase 4)

[Русская версия](rinc-review.md) | [Development and reproducibility](development-en.md)

## Purpose

This procedure establishes accurate `rinc_author_id` and `spin` identifiers
for people in the IndologyScholars archive. RINC profiles may include
namesakes and are not automatically disambiguated, so manual validation is
required to avoid false links.

## Review Queue

The candidate queue is generated by `scratch/rinc_author_candidates.py` and
saved as `analytics_output/rinc_lookup_queue.csv`.

## Review Process

1. Open `rinc_lookup_queue.csv`.
2. Follow the link in the `rinc_search_url` column to search eLIBRARY by surname.
3. Identify the person using multiple signals:
   - Affiliation: does the profile organization match affiliations found in the speaker's talks?
   - Publication subjects: do publications concern Indology, Buddhist studies, Oriental studies, history, linguistics, or philosophy?
   - Active years: do publication years agree with known conference participation?
   - Co-authors: do other known Zograf or Roerich participants occur among them?
4. Complete the CSV fields:
   - Set `review_status` to `confirmed`, `ambiguous`, or `not_found`.
   - Enter `rinc_author_id`, the author ID in the RINC profile URL.
   - Enter `spin` when a SPIN code is shown.
   - Enter the complete confirmed `profile_url`.
   - Enter the reviewer's name or initials in `reviewer`.
   - Enter the review date in `checked_at` as `YYYY-MM-DD`.

## Namesakes and Ambiguity

When several people share a surname and initials, inspect the publication list
in each profile. If a profile mixes relevant Indological publications with an
unrelated field, mark it `ambiguous` and explain the issue in `notes`, or
accept it only with an explicit record of the contamination. Never link a
profile without at least one relevant subject or organizational signal.

## Integration

After a profile has been confirmed (`review_status = confirmed`), transfer its
`rinc_author_id` and `spin` to `authority_ids.json`. On the next site
generation, `generate_scholars_pages.py` will include RINC links on person
profiles.
